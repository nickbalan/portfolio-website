Pros and Cons of enrolling in the Full-Stack Development program offered by CareerFoundry (CF). My Review of the CF program

Pros
  • I had a very motivating and supportive tutor, mentor, and career specialist;
  • CF only issues non-credit certificates after completing the courses, but these are authorized by the ZFU (Staatliche Zentralstelle fÃ¼r Fernunterricht) - Germany's state body for distance education. So no academic training is required to enroll in this program;
  • The alum community is friendly, large, collaborative, and international;
  • Once involved in the CF program, the tutor and the mentor assigned to you will help you create or improve your first portfolio projects;
  • The program includes a "Job Preparation" module which also includes online meetings with a career specialist for six months to prepare and support you in finding a job;
  • Includes a "Job Guarantee" program that supposedly guarantees that you will get a refund if you don't get any job within six months after completing the courses.

Cons
  • The curriculum is not well structured and sometimes creates confusion, which inevitably leads to a delay in meeting the set deadlines for the tasks of each module;
  • The study material/curriculum needs to be updated by at least 2-3 years. Afterward, this outdated curriculum impedes any alumni from finding a job, as most employers are looking for up-to-date employees with new technologies;
  • The program claims that it will teach you web programming languages from scratch and that you won't need any prior programming knowledge; In reality, however, this program is intended for someone who already has at least the basic knowledge in programming languages. Honestly, if I did not have an academic background in computer science, I would not have finished this program because the program does not focus on the basics of programming languages. So, if I were now a newbie looking to enroll in a Web Development program, I wouldn't choose CF;
  • After finishing this program, you will have to improve your knowledge and skills in the studied field by enrolling in other courses or programs that will help you learn and acquire new skills regarding the new technologies that have been developed in the last 2-3 years;
  • The cost of this program in its current state (with an outdated curriculum) is exaggerated; in my opinion, the current program shouldn't cost more than â¬2000 - 2500;
  • The Job Guarantee Program should be revised, I know many alums who did not find a job within 7-12 months (or more) after finishing their courses, but CF did not refund them anything (no dime). Even though their "Job Guarantee" program says that everyone will get a refund if they don't get a job in 6 months after finishing the courses.

If you are interested in learning more about my experience or other experiences of other CF alums, you can contact me on LinkedIn (https://www.linkedin.com/in/nickbalan/).
