Don't ever give up on your dreams

Part I - Mixed origins

/* Monday, February 23th, 2023 // Author: Nick Balan // Category: Motivation */


Hi everyone,


I'm Nick Balan; I was born and raised in Chisinau, Moldova, also in Chisinau I completed a master's degree, then moved to Romania for two years; after that, I moved to Germany, where I have been living for the last six years.


My parents were born in the USSR, but my grandparents were born in Greater Romania. "How is this possible?" you might ask yourself.


According to history, in 1940, the Soviet Union (USSR) issued an ultimatum to Greater Romania (currently Romania) requesting the cession of the territory of Bessarabia (the current Republic of Moldova) and north Bucovina. Thus, the region of Bessarabia (now the Republic of Moldova) and north Bucovina was annexed to the USSR, remaining part of it for half a century until 1991, when the USSR fell apart, and the Republic of Moldova declared its independence from the USSR.


In other words, my grandparents were born in Greater Romania, my parents were born in the USSR, and I was a few years old when the Republic of Moldova declared its independence.


If you are interested in the history of my country of origin, I recommend reading the following Wikipedia pages:
1. Rep. Moldova wiki page, section: World War II and Soviet era;
2. Romania wiki page, section: World War II losses.
